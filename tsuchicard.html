In the Silence, Something Stirs

In a quiet pond lit only by starlight, every koi is asleep, except one. Tsuchi is not like the others. Too dark. Too quiet. Too full of questions no one else dares ask.

While the school chases sunlight and status, he stares past the reeds, dreaming of a world he's never seen. Then an ancient tortoise whispers a forbidden tale: long ago, a humble carp left this very pond, fought the impossible The Great Waterfall, and vanished forever.

What if the legend is real? What if reaching the top remakes you into something more?

Haunted by wonder and a loneliness that aches like an empty current, Tsuchi risks the only home he has ever known. Some journeys demand more than courage. Some demand everything you are.

A Fable for the Restless

A luminous, devastating meditation on friendship and transformation. A story where beauty isn't decoration—it's the entire architecture of meaning.

For readers who've ever felt too different. Too quiet. Too full of impossible dreams. Tsuchi is the book that whispers: your restlessness is not a flaw. Your hunger for something more is not a curse. It's the beginning of your becoming.

A tale that lingers like memory. A truth that resonates long after the final page.
